# Vibe Code Cup — Master Plan

**Status:** Working master plan  
**Date:** 2026-03-26

## 1. Objective

Launch a premium, builder-first AI coding competition that feels credible to top technical builders, attractive to sponsors, and compelling to watch live.

Core public framing:

> **Where the best builders, workflows, and tools rise to the top.**

## 3. Event thesis

Vibe Code Cup should sit at the intersection of:
- technical credibility
- sponsor-friendly packaging
- repeatable competition infrastructure

The point is not just to run a hackathon. The point is to create a format that:
- surfaces strong builders and workflows under pressure
- creates good livestream moments
- generates clips, recaps, and sponsor inventory
- can evolve into recurring rounds or seasons

## 4. Pilot event format

### Recommended pilot structure

**Live kickoff + asynchronous build + same-day results/finale**

Flow:
1. Pre-event marketing and signups (registration is fully open)
2. Kickoff livestream reveals the challenge
3. Competitors get 90 minutes to build and submit (one single submission and judging phase)
4. Submissions close at deadline
5. Harness validates and scores entries offline
6. Finalists / winners are revealed on an entertaining same-day livestream featuring post-match analysis, workflow discussion, and athlete interviews

### Why this is the right pilot shape

- simpler to run than a fully live all-day tournament
- preserves suspense because the challenge is secret until kickoff
- gives online participants equal footing
- still creates a strong content arc for livestream and clips
- lets the judging system do real work without requiring chaos on stream

## 6. Audience strategy

The point of this format is to show what workflows and tools are actually effective in practice, and to demonstrate what the future of competitive coding looks like when AI tools are allowed.

The landing page and outreach should speak to three groups at once.

### Builders
They want:
- a hard challenge
- prestige and bragging rights
- freedom to use their workflow and tools
- a format that rewards execution

Messaging angle:
- Bring your tools. Show your workflow. Prove it.

### Viewers
They want:
- recognizable builders
- a simple, high-stakes format
- visible competition and time pressure
- a fun finale with commentary and reveals

Messaging angle:
- live kickoff, ticking clock, builder showdown

### Sponsors
They want:
- real technical audience access
- brand-safe but exciting event packaging
- recurring content inventory
- a credible, repeatable format

Messaging angle:
- direct access to builders, technical viewers, and hiring managers
- sponsor visibility across event assets, stream, recap, and social clips
